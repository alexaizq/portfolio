    About the project

    During this makeathon challenge, we were tasked with creating something incorporating the makerspace mascot, the Calihorn. This was a great opportunity to incorporate a fun mechanism design, constrained by materials and manufacturing processes. We chose to make a pinball machine.

    I worked on the mechanism design and through brainstorming and online research we came up with the a flipper that is spring loaded with a rubber band and constraining the range of motion though the design of the support. Since we were working with 3mm or 6mm plywood, in order to create shafts with a higher stiffness, to reduce undesirable deflection, that are easy to assemble, I made a + sign cross-section using a 2D geometry that you can easily link together.

    Lessons learned

    While a rubber band is fairly easy to get, and it works when glued to the side of the frame, a more elegant and robust solution could use a torsion spring to fulfill the same purpose. It is also worth exploring a more robust attachment of the rubber band to the frame. I would love to work on adding electromechanical bumpers to make the machine more interactive and unpredictable, as well as packaging the design into an open source kit.
